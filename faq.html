What is an EPC?

It’s a legal requirement to get an EPC every time you buy, sell or rent a property. The EPC shows you the estimated heating, lighting and hot water costs, and carbon emissions released over three years. The EPC gives you recommendations about how you can reduce energy use and costs. With an EPC you can compare the energy efficiency ratings of homes across the UK.

Is it a legal requirement to have an EPC?

Yes, since 2009 in Scotland is it law that an EPC is every time a home is put up for sale or rent an EPC must be provided.

How long is an EPC certificate valid for ?

An EPC is valid for 10 years and can be used multiple times during this period. However the EPC has to accurately reflect the condition of your property at the time therefore if you have had construction work or made improvements to the heating, windows or insulation it would be advisable to get a new EPC done.

What are the recent changes to EPC requirements?

From 1 April 2018 there will be a requirement for new lets and renewals of properties rented out in the private sector to have a minimum energy efficiency rating of E on an EPC. From 1 April 2020 the minimum standard of an energy efficiency rating of E on an EPC will apply to all existing tenancies. It will be unlawful to rent a property which breaches the requirement for a minimum E rating, unless there is an applicable exemption. Refer to the Energy Performance of Buildings (England and Wales) Regulations 2012 and Energy Performance of Buildings (Scotland) Regulations 2008 for detailed information.
This is not law in Scotland yet, the government is in discussions over it
