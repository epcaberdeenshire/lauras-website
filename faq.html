FAQ

What is an EPC?

An Energy Performance Certificate is required by law when you build, sell or rent a property. It gives the property an energy efficiency rating and recommendations how to reduce energy, save money and increase your rating. It is valid for 10 years.

What is involved?

You will need an accredited Domestic Energy Assessor to carry out your EPC. The assessment should take less than an hour. They will need access to all the rooms and the loft if possible. They take measurements, inside and outside the property, inspect heating systems and take photos of things like the boiler. The assessment is non invasive. The assessor then inputs all the data and produces an Energy Performance Certificate which is lodged on the central register.


I'm a landlord, do I need an EPC?

Yes, you cannot lease out a property without an EPC. Check the cost of the company that your agent uses as they are often not very competitive. 

I have an old EPC but have made home improvements. Can it be updated?

A new EPC test would have to be done, This is a must if you're selling or renting even if you old EPC is still in date. You will improve your energy efficiency rating making the property much more appealing to house hunters.

I own my house but am not thinking of buying, do I still need an EPC?

No you don't BUT having an EPC carried out on your property can point out areas where energy efficiency can be improved and thus save you money.
